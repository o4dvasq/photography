# Oscar's Photography Curriculum
*Last updated: March 2026 | Coach: Claude (Anthropic)*

---

## Philosophy

This program prioritizes enjoyment first, skill-building second. The structure exists to create opportunities, not obligations. San Francisco is the classroom — its light, neighborhoods, fog, and dramatic terrain are the curriculum. Technique is woven in naturally, not imposed.

No daily frame limits. No pressure. When you have something worth looking at, we look at it.

---

## The System

### Morning Check-In
Each morning, say "morning check-in" and Claude will:
- Note current SF weather and light conditions
- Flag any special opportunities (fog burn-off, offshore flow, golden hour timing, storm light)
- Remind you of active lens and compositional theme
- Suggest specific locations on your commute or accessible that day

### Shooting Windows (Weekdays)
- **Morning:** Commute through Forest Hill → Presidio → Golden Gate Bridge
- **Afternoon/Evening:** Outer Sunset, Inner Sunset accessible after work; sunset/golden hour primary target
- **Core locations:** Forest Hill neighborhood, Presidio, Golden Gate Bridge (both sides), Outer Sunset, Inner Sunset

### Weekends
Neighborhood exploration assignments (Option 1 overlay). One neighborhood per month with a location-specific brief tied to that neighborhood's actual visual character.

## Compositional Theme Rotation

Themes cycle weekly. Each theme is intentionally paired with the lens that teaches it best. A new theme kicks off each Monday with:
- A recommended article to read
- A photographer to study

| Theme | Best Lens Pairing | Why |
|-------|------------------|-----|
| **Leading Lines** | 35mm | Natural FOV reads lines honestly, no distortion drama |
| **Negative Space** | 18mm | Wide angle generates breathing room naturally |
| **Framing Within a Frame** | 75mm | Compression lets you isolate frames cleanly |
| **Diagonals** | 16-50mm | Zoom lets you find and refine diagonal energy |
| **Compression & Layers** | 50-200mm | Telephoto is the native tool for stacked depth |
| **Figure/Ground** | 35mm | Subject/background at normal FOV, no tricks |
| **Light & Shadow as Shape** | 18mm | Wide angle captures shadow geometry in full context |
| **Pattern & Repetition** | 75mm | Medium tele flattens scenes and reveals structure |

## Feedback Rubric

Feedback is given per image, keyed to filename. The rubric grows as new themes are introduced. Only active criteria are evaluated each week — no retroactive grading on themes not yet covered.

### Current Rubric (Week 1–2)

**1. Leading Lines**
- Is there a clear line or path guiding the eye?
- Where does the line lead — to a subject, a vanishing point, or nowhere?
- Does the line enter the frame intentionally (usually from an edge or corner)?
- Is the line's direction working for or against the composition?
- Diagonal lines: do they add energy? Converging lines: do they create depth?

*Scale: Not present / Attempted but ineffective / Working / Strong*
